Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, starting decades of innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel releases the 4004, the first commercial microprocessor.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 launches, establishing the x86 architecture for PCs and servers.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386, a 32-bit processor that boosted PC performance.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel's greenhouse gas emissions peak; the company then invests in renewables and efficiency.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches the RISE strategy and 2030 sustainability goals.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel commits to net-zero emissions by 2040 across its operations.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit with suppliers, officials, and industry leaders.
